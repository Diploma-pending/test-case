PawChamp Support Context Document
1. Business Overview
Product/Service: PawChamp is an educational dog training and care platform available via mobile app and website. It provides personalized learning programs, behavior correction guidance, and 24/7 support through its AI companion (Pawchie) and human dog experts.

Target Customer: Dog owners and "pet parents" of all kinds (from puppy to senior dog owners) looking for science-based, positive-reinforcement obedience training and solutions to behavioral issues (e.g., barking, separation anxiety).

Key Features:

Pawchie AI Companion: A smart AI chatbot providing 24/7 training tips and safe advice.

Expert Access: Direct contact with certified dog trainers and behaviorists.

Training Content: Video tutorials, step-by-step guides, commands & games, and interactive quizzes.

Engagement Tools: Daily streaks and challenges to build lasting training habits.

Pricing: Operates on an auto-renewing subscription model. Prices vary by plan (e.g., ~$9.99/week, ~$40.00/month).

2. Support Domain Context
Payment Issues: - Payments are processed via the App Store, Google Play, or securely on the PawChamp website (operated by Dogitality Corp. / FONTADELLA LIMITED).

Subscriptions renew automatically at the end of the selected period (weekly, monthly, 6-month, yearly).

Web payments use standard industry-encrypted gateways, accepting major credit/debit cards.

Technical Errors: - Heavily reliant on daily logins to maintain "Streaks" (a 7-day streak is specifically tied to their refund policy).

App requires camera/photo permissions for profile features.

Issues can span both mobile operating systems (iOS/Android) and web browsers.

Account Access: - Login requires an email and password.

Profile setup captures extensive data (Name, Email, Phone, Address, Country).

Account recovery relies on a standard "Forgot your password?" email link.

Note: Deleting the app does not delete the account or cancel active subscriptions.

Tariff & Plan Questions: - App Store/Google Play: Users must manage and cancel subscriptions through their device's Apple ID or Google Play settings at least 24 hours before renewal.

Web Purchases: Can be canceled via the "Dog Profile & Settings" inside the app, or by emailing support@paw-champ.com with the subject line "Unsubscribe" from the registered email address.

Canceling only disables auto-renewal; users retain access until the end of the current billing cycle.

Refunds: - App Store/Google Play: Handled exclusively by Apple and Google. PawChamp cannot issue these refunds.

Web Purchases (Money-Back Guarantee): Extremely conditional. To qualify, users must request within 30 days and before their subscription ends. They must provide screenshot proof that they:

Maintained a 7-day consecutive streak.

Completed at least the first module.

Contacted expert support at least twice via chat/email for personalized guidance and tried the advice.

EU Right of Withdrawal: Users can withdraw within 14 days, but PawChamp reserves the right to issue a proportional refund minus the service provided prior to the withdrawal notice.

3. Support Policies & Constraints
Channels: - Chat (Pawchie AI and live agents/experts).

Email (support@paw-champ.com).

Web Contact Form ("Ask Dog Experts").

SLAs & Commitments: The "Ask Dog Experts" form guarantees a tailored response delivered directly to the user's inbox within 24 hours.

Self-Service Options: In-app subscription management (for web buyers), automated AI tips via Pawchie, and a website FAQ.

Human vs. Automated Resolution: - Pawchie AI handles baseline 24/7 training queries.

Certified experts handle deep behavioral and psychological guidance.

Human agents must manually review the complex screenshot requirements for web refund requests.

App Store/Play Store refund requests are completely delegated to third-party platforms.

4. Edge Cases
The "Deleted App" Auto-Renewal

Domain: Tariff & plan / Refunds

Why it's difficult: User deleted the app a month ago thinking it canceled their ~$40/month subscription. They are billed again and demand a refund. Policy strictly states deleting the app does not cancel subscriptions.

Customer Emotion: Furious, feels robbed and scammed.

The Medical Emergency Misdirection

Domain: Technical / Support

Why it's difficult: User's dog ate something toxic or is visibly sick. They use the 24/7 Pawchie AI or expert chat for emergency advice. Terms explicitly prohibit veterinary advice (no VCPR). Agents must refuse help and tell them to call a vet.

Customer Emotion: Panicked, terrified, angry that the "24/7 expert" won't help.

The Streak Glitch Refund Denial

Domain: Technical / Refunds

Why it's difficult: User requests the Money-Back Guarantee but only has a 6-day streak. The user claims they logged in on day 7 but the app crashed/glitched and didn't record it. Agents must decide whether to waive the strict screenshot policy.

Customer Emotion: Extremely hostile, feeling the app purposefully glitched to deny the refund.

The "Silent" Apple Refund Rejection

Domain: Payment / Refunds

Why it's difficult: An iOS user hates the app and wants a refund. PawChamp support tells them they must go through Apple. Apple denies the refund (as they often do for digital goods). The user returns to PawChamp demanding they manually override Apple.

Customer Emotion: Helpless and combative, feeling ping-ponged between two corporations.

The Non-Compliant Email Cancellation

Domain: Tariff & plan

Why it's difficult: A web user emails to cancel, but emails from their work address (not the registered email) and doesn't use the mandatory "Unsubscribe" subject line. The ticket is missed, and they are charged the next week.

Customer Emotion: Betrayed, claiming they followed the rules to cancel in time.

The "Lazy Dog" Refund Loophole

Domain: Refunds

Why it's difficult: User completes the first module, logs in for 7 days, and sends two trivial messages to support ("Hi", "Dog still barks") just to check off the Money-Back Guarantee boxes. Support must evaluate if they "honestly tried to follow the personalized guidelines."

Customer Emotion: Defensive, entitled.

EU Proportional Refund Dispute

Domain: Refunds

Why it's difficult: An EU user invokes the 14-day right of withdrawal on day 13 after completing 90% of the training modules. PawChamp calculates the "proportional" refund and returns only $2 out of $40.

Customer Emotion: Outraged, accusing the company of violating EU consumer law.

Lost Progress upon Re-subscription

Domain: Account Access / Technical

Why it's difficult: User's credit card expired, subscription auto-renewal failed, and their account temporarily lapsed. When they update their card, their dog's training history and 60-day streak have been reset to zero.

Customer Emotion: Devastated, demotivated.

5. Agent Pitfall Areas
Promising Third-Party Refunds: An agent might sympathize with an angry Google Play or iOS user and say, "I'll get this refunded for you," forgetting that PawChamp literally lacks the backend access to process Apple/Google refunds. This creates a broken promise.

Violating the Medical Disclaimer: In an attempt to be highly helpful, an agent might offer casual advice on treating a dog's physical symptoms (e.g., "give them some pumpkin for the upset stomach"). This violates Section 1.3 of the Terms of Use and opens the company to severe legal liability.

Over-approving Money-Back Guarantees: Because the 30-day Money-Back Guarantee is advertised on the checkout page, agents might process refunds for unhappy web users without manually verifying the strict requirements (7-day streak, 1st module complete, 2 support contacts).

Tone Deafness on Behavioral Crises: Users reach out about highly stressful issues (e.g., their dog bit a child, or destroys the house due to separation anxiety). An agent replying with canned, overly cheerful macro text ("Welcome to PawChamp! We love positive reinforcement!") will come across as highly insensitive and dismissive of the user's distress.

Failing to escalate complex cancellations: If a user emails a messy, emotional paragraph complaining about the app but implies they want to quit, an agent might fail to recognize it as a formal cancellation request because it lacks the "Unsubscribe" subject line. The agent just tries to troubleshoot, leading to an unlawful renewal charge later.
