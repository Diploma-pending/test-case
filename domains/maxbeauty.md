MaxBeauty Support Context Document
1. Business Overview
Product/Service: MaxBeauty is an app-based, AI-powered personalized beauty care program. It offers a holistic approach to self-care, providing courses and daily routines for skincare, face yoga, hair care, and diet. Specific offerings include the "Korean Beauty Plan," the "Fascia Release Program," and the "Routine Mastery Calendar." The company differentiates itself by basing routines on public clinical guidelines and peer-reviewed findings (e.g., AAD, JAAD) rather than fleeting trends.

Target Customer: Beauty and self-care enthusiasts looking for structured, science-backed routines that adapt to their specific needs, skin type, lifestyle, diet, and even menstrual cycle or local seasons.

Key Features & Pricing: Features include an AI Assistant available 24/7, personalized routines, 200+ lessons, printables/handbooks, and tailored product recommendations. Subscriptions start at $19.99/month.

2. Support Domain Context
Payment issues: MaxBeauty operates on a recurring monthly subscription model ($19.99/month minimum). Payment flows likely involve direct credit/debit card processing via the website, as well as potential in-app purchases through the Apple App Store. Failed payments result in a loss of premium access to personalized routines and the AI Assistant.

Technical errors: Common failure points involve the MaxBeauty App (video playback issues across 200+ lessons), synchronization glitches with the Routine Mastery Calendar, or the AI Assistant failing to generate appropriate responses based on holistic inputs (diet, menstrual cycle).

Account access: Users log in via the app or web platform. Account access is crucial for maintaining the streak required for the money-back guarantee. Issues include standard password resets, lost progress/data on the holistic tracker, and syncing issues across devices.

Tariff & plan questions: Clarifying the value of the $19.99/month subscription versus traditional skincare or in-store consultations. Users may ask about the differences between specific tracks, like the "Korean Beauty Plan" versus the "MaxBeauty Plan."

Refunds: MaxBeauty has a highly specific Money-Back Guarantee:

Requests must be submitted via email to support@themaxbeauty.com within 30 days of the initial purchase and before the end of the subscription period.

Crucial Condition: The user must demonstrate they used MaxBeauty for at least 14 consecutive days by providing relevant screenshots or other confirmation.

EU Residents: Have a 14-day withdrawal right, but expressly lose this right once the digital content is delivered/validated (unless the content is defective).

US Residents: Certain states allow a refund if the purchase is canceled prior to midnight of the third business day after the purchase.

4. Edge Cases
The "13-Day" Refund Denial (Refunds): A customer requests a refund under the 30-day guarantee but provides screenshots showing only 13 consecutive days of app usage instead of the required 14. Emotion: Extremely frustrated and feeling scammed by a technicality.

App Store Billing Mismatch (Payment Issues): A user emails support to cancel and refund their $19.99 subscription, but they purchased it via the Apple App Store. The agent cannot process the refund directly and must redirect the user to Apple. Emotion: Annoyed and impatient about the runaround.

EU Right of Withdrawal Dispute (Refunds): An EU user demands a refund within 5 days of purchase citing EU consumer law. The agent must explain that by immediately accessing the personalized digital content, they waived this right per the Terms. Emotion: Hostile and legally combative.

Menstrual Cycle Sync Failure (Technical Errors): A user relying on the "Holistic Approach" feature reports that the app deleted their menstrual cycle data, resulting in wildly inaccurate skincare recommendations from the AI. Emotion: Violated, concerned about data privacy, and confused.

Misunderstood "Expert Help" (Tariffs & Plans): A customer subscribes expecting 1-on-1 video consultations with a licensed dermatologist based on the phrasing "Expert help is only a click away," but realizes they are interacting with an AI Assistant. Emotion: Deceived and demanding an immediate refund.

Lost Printable Handbooks (Account Access): A user cannot find the download links for their Routine Planners and handbooks after switching to a new device, preventing them from maintaining the "gentle structure" they paid for. Emotion: Anxious and disorganized.

US 3-Day Cancellation Window Missed by Hours (Refunds): A resident of a qualifying US state emails their cancellation request at 1:00 AM on the fourth business day, technically missing the midnight deadline for the no-questions-asked refund. Emotion: Desperate and argumentative.

Conflicting AI vs. AAD Advice (Technical Errors): A user notices the AI Assistant recommends an exfoliating product that contradicts the publicized American Academy of Dermatology (AAD) guidelines for their specific rosacea skin type, challenging the app's "Science-Based" claim. Emotion: Distrustful and angry.

5. Agent Pitfall Areas
Refunding without Verifying the 14-Day Streak: An agent sees a refund request within the 30-day window and processes it immediately, failing to ask the user for the mandatory screenshots proving 14 consecutive days of usage. This violates standard operating procedure and loses revenue unnecessarily.

Giving Medical or Dermatological Advice: A user contacts support with a severe skin reaction to a recommended routine. An agent attempts to troubleshoot the reaction or offer skincare advice instead of directing them to a real doctor and clarifying that MaxBeauty (and its support team) provides cosmetic guidance, not medical treatment.

Mishandling EU Refund Rejections: An agent bluntly denies an EU refund without clearly explaining why (that the user consented to immediate digital delivery, thereby waiving the 14-day withdrawal right). Poor phrasing here can lead to escalated legal or regulatory complaints.

Over-promising AI Capabilities: When a user reports a bug with the personalized routine, an agent promises that a "dermatologist will review your profile," fundamentally misrepresenting the AI-driven nature of the $19.99/month service.

Ignoring the US State-Specific 3-Day Rule: A customer from a qualifying US state requests a refund on day 2. An agent blindly applies the standard policy (demanding 14 days of usage screenshots) and denies the refund, violating state law and the company's own Money-Back Guarantee addendum.
