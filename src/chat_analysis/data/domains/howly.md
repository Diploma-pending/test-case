Howly Context Document
1. Business Overview
Product/Service: Howly is an online platform that provides on-demand, 24/7 personalized consultations with verified experts (such as IT engineers, appliance technicians, car mechanics, software specialists, and hardware experts) via chat.

Target Customer: Individuals seeking immediate troubleshooting or professional advice for everyday technical, mechanical, or household issues without the cost or wait time of an in-person appointment.

Key Features & Pricing:

Subscription Model: The service operates on a recurring subscription, not a one-time fee.

Pricing: Subscriptions cost from $46/month (specifically billed every 28 days).

Join Fee: Users must pay a one-time "join fee" alongside their first monthly payment at the sign-up stage.

Paid Trial: A 7-day paid trial (often heavily discounted, e.g., $1) is offered, which automatically converts to the full $46/month subscription if not canceled.

Feature Access: Active subscribers receive unlimited consultations and can fix an unlimited number of issues. Experts reportedly respond in an average of 37 seconds.

2. Support Domain Context
Payment issues:

Accepted methods: Credit and debit cards processed via third-party providers.

Billing cycles: Automatic renewal every 28 days. Both the initial monthly fee and the one-time join fee are charged immediately upon sign-up or after the 7-day trial period expires.

Failed payment behavior: If auto-renewal fails, the user’s access is restricted. When they attempt to ask a new question, they will hit a paywall and be prompted to update their payment method or resubscribe.

Technical errors:

Common failure points: The platform heavily relies on "magic link" email logins. Users frequently experience login failures when the secure sign-in email is routed to their Spam/Junk folder, or if the link expires before they click it.

Integrations: Support is integrated with Zendesk, Customer.io, and secure payment processors.

Platform support: Fully web-based; users interact with experts primarily via mobile or desktop browser chat.

Account access:

Login methods: Passwordless entry. Users enter their email address on the Sign-In page, and Howly sends a secure sign-in link.

Account recovery: Standard recovery involves resending the magic link.

Suspension/ban policies: Howly reserves the right to cancel a subscription and terminate access if the user fails to pay, initiates a chargeback, or violates the Terms of Use.

Tariff & plan questions:

Plan differences: The primary differentiator is the trial phase versus the active subscription phase.

Upgrade/Downgrade: If a user cancels, their access remains active until the end of the current billing period. They can easily reactivate by clicking "Resubscribe" in the Membership tab.

Feature limits: There are no limits on the number of questions or expert interactions during an active subscription.

Refunds:

Eligibility: Refunds can be requested within 7 days from the date of initial registration. If eligible, Howly refunds both the Subscription Fee and the one-time join fee.

Partial refunds: EU residents have a 14-day right of withdrawal; however, because the service is provided on a continuous basis, Howly deducts a proportionate amount for the service already used before issuing the refund.

Chargeback policies: "Subscription traps" are a common complaint. If users file chargebacks (e.g., claiming they didn't authorize the recurring $46 charge), Howly will lock the account.

If a user is dissatisfied with an expert's advice (indicated by a 1-star rating), the issue is escalated to Quality Control and Customer Care, who must offer to connect the user with a different expert before defaulting to cancellation.

Self-Service Options: Users can self-cancel by navigating to "My Account" -> "Payment & subscription" (or "Membership") -> "Cancel Subscription". A comprehensive Help Center/FAQ is also available.

Human vs. Auto: System handles automated billing, auto-renewals, and magic link generation. Human agents handle expert reassignment, refund approvals (especially calculating proportionate EU refunds), and managing duplicate charge disputes.

4. Edge Cases
Domain: Refunds / Billing

Scenario: A user signed up for a $1 trial, forgot about it, and was billed $46 on day 8. They request a refund on day 9.

Why it’s difficult: The policy strictly states refunds are only valid within 7 days of registration. The agent must deny the refund for a service the user feels they didn't use.

Customer Emotion: Furious, feeling scammed/trapped.

Domain: Account Access

Scenario: A user made a typo in their email address during sign-up. They were charged the join fee but cannot receive the "secure sign-in link" to use the service or cancel the subscription.

Why it’s difficult: The user cannot authenticate themselves via the standard self-service flow, requiring manual agent intervention to locate the payment via partial card details and correct the email.

Customer Emotion: Panicked and frustrated.

Domain: Support Routing / Tech Errors

Scenario: An appliance technician expert gives advice that results in the user accidentally breaking their washing machine further.

Why it’s difficult: The Terms of Use explicitly disclaim warranties regarding the accuracy or reliability of expert advice. The agent must handle liability claims while attempting to de-escalate.

Customer Emotion: Extremely hostile and litigious.

Domain: Refunds / Geography

Scenario: An EU user demands a full refund on day 12 under their 14-day legal right of withdrawal, but they have already successfully resolved 4 technical issues with experts.

Why it’s difficult: The agent cannot give a full refund; they must manually calculate a "proportionate deduction" for the 4 resolved issues, which the user will likely dispute.

Customer Emotion: Argumentative and entitled.

Domain: Payment Issues

Scenario: A user attempts to ask a follow-up question but hits a paywall because their $46 auto-renewal failed. They just deposited money and want to push the payment through immediately to finish fixing their car.

Why it’s difficult: The agent may not have a "force charge" button and must guide the user to update their payment method to re-trigger the automated billing cycle.

Customer Emotion: Anxious and impatient.

Domain: Account Access / Billing

Scenario: An older user didn't understand how to log back in. Instead of using the magic link, they opened a new browser tab, created a new account, and paid the trial fee + join fee again to ask a second question.

Why it’s difficult: The user has accidentally created duplicate active subscriptions and wants historical charges refunded, which crosses over standard refund policy limits.

Customer Emotion: Confused and overwhelmed.

Domain: Tariffs & Plans

Scenario: A user from California cancels on the 4th business day and demands a full refund, citing California consumer protection laws.

Why it’s difficult: The Howly Terms specify California residents can cancel without penalty prior to midnight of the third business day following registration. The agent must carefully explain the specific legal cutoff.

Customer Emotion: Righteous and confrontational.

Domain: Payment Issues

Scenario: A user canceled their debit card to stop the Howly subscription without properly canceling on the site. Their bank used "Account Updater" services to automatically provide Howly with the new card number, resulting in a successful $46 charge.

Why it’s difficult: The user is convinced Howly "hacked" their new card, requiring the agent to explain banking network auto-update protocols rather than Howly's systems.

Customer Emotion: Highly suspicious and angry.

5. Agent Pitfall Areas
Refusing to Refund the "Join Fee": During a valid 7-day refund request, an agent might only refund the recurring subscription fee ($46) and forget that the policy explicitly requires refunding the one-time "join fee" alongside it.

Failing to Attempt Expert Reassignment: When a user complains that their consultation was useless, an agent's first instinct might be to process a cancellation. The required SOP is to apologize and offer to connect the user with a different expert to resolve the issue and save the subscription.

Mismanaging the "7-Day" Policy vs. Local Laws: An agent might blanket-deny a refund on day 10, failing to check the user's location. If the user is in the EU (14-day proportionate withdrawal right) or Brazil (7-day cooling-off from the date of contract which might differ slightly in interpretation), the agent creates a legal compliance failure.

Sending Generic "Check Spam" Macros: When a user complains they aren't getting the login link, an agent might repeatedly send the "check your spam folder" macro without verifying if the email address on file contains a typo, leaving the user permanently locked out.
