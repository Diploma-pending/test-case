Brighterly Support Context Document
1. Business Overview
Product/Service: Brighterly is an EdTech platform providing 1-on-1 online math and reading (ELA) tutoring for K-12 students. The curriculum is aligned with US Common Core standards and utilizes a gamified, interactive digital whiteboard environment.

Target Customer: Parents in the US with children in elementary and middle school (primarily grades 1–9) who want their kids to catch up, build confidence, or accelerate their STEM and literacy skills.

Key Features: 45-minute individualized video sessions with expert tutors, free diagnostic demo lessons, regular parental progress reports, printable worksheets, and flexible scheduling.

Pricing & Subscription Plans: Pricing is package-based and starts at approximately $17.30–$17.40 per lesson. Subscriptions are structured as "Learning Memberships" of 1, 3, 6, or 12 months, where parents select a frequency of 1 to 3 classes per week. As an example, a 1-month plan with 2 lessons/week costs around $211/month.

2. Support Domain Context
Payment Issues

Accepted Methods: All prices are in USD. Payments are processed via Stripe and SolidGate.

Billing Cycles: All subscription plans are subject to automatic renewal (auto-renewal).

Cancellation Flow: To cancel auto-renewal, the user must submit a request to the Support Team no later than 3 business days before the scheduled renewal date.

Technical Errors

Platform: Browser-based virtual classroom with video, audio, and an interactive digital whiteboard. Accessible on any device.

Common Failures: Time zone syncing issues during scheduling, audio/video connectivity drops, and missed email links to the Teacher Room portal.

Account Access

Login Methods: Web-based login using credentials created within 24 hours of purchasing a package.

Policies: The "User" must be a legally responsible adult (18+). Minors ("Students") are strictly prohibited from holding their own accounts. Sharing login information with third parties is a violation of the Terms of Service and results in immediate blocking of access.

Tariff & Plan Questions

Plan Differences: Plans vary strictly by commitment length (1 to 12 months) and lesson frequency (1 to 3 times weekly). Longer commitments reduce the per-lesson rate.

Trial Periods: Brighterly offers a 100% free demo lesson and diagnostic test before requiring a credit card or subscription commitment.

Refunds

General Rule: No automatic refunds. Brighterly has a strict refund policy, and classes awarded at no cost (promotional funds/credits) are strictly non-refundable.

Missed/Late Classes: If a student is late, the time is deducted from the 45-minute class. If the student is a no-show for 15 minutes, the class is canceled with no refund.

Exceptional Circumstances: Refunds for unused lessons are only granted under specific conditions: Teacher Mismatch, Service Quality Issues, Lack of Progress, Family Emergencies, or Lesson Pausing Option.

Time Windows: Refund requests must be made at least 72 hours before the next charge. Processing takes at least 5 business days back to the original payment method. Brighterly reserves the right to request proof (e.g., a medical certificate).

Agents are also trained to offer a "pause" on the subscription rather than a straight cancellation.

4. Edge Cases
Domain: Refunds / Payment Issues

Scenario: A parent's auto-renewal triggers, and they email 24 hours prior to the billing date to cancel. They are charged and demand a refund.

Difficulty: The policy explicitly requires 3 business days' notice. The agent must enforce the charge for the upcoming month while legally fulfilling the cancellation for the subsequent month.

Customer Emotion: Frustrated and hostile.

Domain: Tariff & Plan Questions

Scenario: A parent agrees to "pause" their 6-month subscription instead of canceling it based on an agent's recommendation. Two months later, the pause expires automatically, the card is charged, and the parent claims it was an unauthorized charge.

Difficulty: Ambiguity around how "pausing" works vs. definitive cancellation. The parent feels misled by the retention tactic.

Customer Emotion: Angry and feeling scammed.

Domain: Technical Errors

Scenario: A student is 16 minutes late to the virtual classroom because their local internet provider had an outage. The tutor closed the room at the 15-minute mark (per policy).

Difficulty: The parent wants a free makeup class because the delay wasn't their fault, but the system logged it as a chargeable no-show.

Customer Emotion: Highly frustrated and helpless.

Domain: Refunds

Scenario: A parent claims a "Teacher Mismatch" under the Exceptional Circumstances policy, stating the tutor's accent is too difficult for their 1st grader to understand, and demands a full retroactive refund for the 4 classes already taken.

Difficulty: Refunds only apply to unused lessons, even under exceptional circumstances. The agent must deny the refund for past classes while assigning a new tutor for the remaining ones.

Customer Emotion: Combative and feeling unheard.

Domain: Account Access

Scenario: A 13-year-old student emails support from the parent's email address to cancel an upcoming class (under the 6-hour window) to avoid a test.

Difficulty: Minors cannot act as the "User." The agent must verify if the request came from the adult, which may involve calling the parent and exposing the child's actions.

Customer Emotion: The child is anxious/mischievous; the parent (once informed) will likely be embarrassed or annoyed.

Domain: Refunds

Scenario: A parent requests a refund for remaining classes due to a "Family Emergency" but gets highly offended when the agent requests supporting documentation (like a medical certificate) as dictated by the ToS.

Difficulty: Balancing empathy with strict corporate policy. Requesting medical proof during a real tragedy can severely damage the brand's reputation.

Customer Emotion: Deeply insulted and grieving/stressed.

Domain: Payment Issues

Scenario: A user attempts to "cash out" or request a refund to their credit card for promotional credits applied to their account during a holiday sale after they decide to switch to a competitor like Kumon.

Difficulty: Promotional funds strictly remain the property of the Service and are never refundable.

Customer Emotion: Entitled and argumentative.

Domain: Technical Errors

Scenario: A parent traveling in a different time zone books a class on their phone. A glitch or user error causes the calendar invite to be 3 hours off. They miss the class and are penalized.

Difficulty: Determining if the fault lies with the Brighterly platform's timezone sync or the user's device settings to decide on a fee waiver.

Customer Emotion: Confused and defensive.

5. Agent Pitfall Areas
Waiving the 6-Hour Rule Too Easily: Agents might feel empathetic to a parent whose child suddenly fell ill and grant a free reschedule without the required 6-hour notice. While good for customer service, this violates ToS and costs the independent tutor their pay for that time slot.

Failing the Retention "Pause" Flow: An agent might immediately process a subscription cancellation upon request instead of following Brighterly's business retention protocol, which involves offering the "Lesson Pausing Option" or adjusting the frequency of lessons to save the account.

Miscalculating the 3-Business-Day Window: An agent might improperly calculate "3 business days" (e.g., counting weekends or holidays) and wrongly deny a valid cancellation request, leading the parent to initiate a Stripe chargeback.

Promising Permanent Tutor Availability: Because tutors are independent contractors (many operating from different global time zones like the Philippines), an agent might guarantee a parent that they can have "Tutor Camille" every Tuesday for the next 12 months. If the tutor's availability changes, the business faces a "Teacher Mismatch" refund liability.

Mishandling Tone When Requesting Proof: When a parent claims a family emergency to break a contract, an agent might use overly robotic, templated language to demand a medical certificate. This lack of tact in high-stress situations frequently leads to negative Trustpilot reviews and escalated supervisor complaints.
