# Support Context Document: Relatio

## 1. Business Overview

* **Product & Service:** Relatio is a mobile application (iOS and Android) focused on relationships, intimacy improvement, and breakup healing. The core experience provides personalized daily 5–10 minute guided courses combining text lessons, audio, videos, and physical exercises (including kegel and pelvic floor exercises, breathwork, and meditations). It also features an "AI chat support" tool for on-demand guidance.
* **Target Customer:** Individuals (singles) or couples experiencing relationship struggles, looking to respectfully reconnect after a breakup, or seeking natural ways to improve intimate performance (e.g., addressing stamina, ED, or premature ejaculation).
* **Pricing & Subscription Model:** Relatio uses a **subscription-based model** where prices vary depending on the chosen term length (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annual). Users pay to access their personalized daily plan and the full library of exercises and courses.

## 2. Support Domain Context

* **Payment Issues:** - *Billing Cycles:* Subscriptions automatically renew based on the selected term length.
* *Cancellations:* To stop future charges, users must explicitly cancel their subscription. The recognized flow is strictly through the app interface: `Profile (bottom right) → Settings icon (top right) → Manage Subscription → Cancel Subscription`.


* **Technical Errors:** - *Platform:* Operates via the Apple App Store and Google Play.
* *Common Failure Points:* Issues with the embedded "AI chat support" generating errors, audio/video playback buffering during guided meditations, syncing progress for daily 5-10 minute tasks, or the initial onboarding "Quick quiz" crashing before generating a plan.


* **Account Access:** - *Account Modes:* Can be used solo or with a partner invited to the app.
* *Data Privacy:* Highly sensitive. The company operates a strict "Privacy-first" policy, meaning users control what is shared, and the app explicitly states it *never* messages partners or exes on the user's behalf.


* **Tariff & Plan Questions:** - *Plan Differences:* The user's specific journey (Reconnection, Intimacy, Performance, or Healing) is determined by the onboarding quiz, but access is universal under the subscription.
* **Refunds:** - *Policy:* Cancellation stops *future* charges ("no further charges will apply"). However, refund policies for past charges generally depend on the platform of purchase (Apple App Store / Google Play) or strict web-subscription Terms of Use. Because the product delivers digital content immediately, refunds are typically limited or require human escalation.

## 3. Support Policies & Constraints

* **Support Channels:** Strictly email via `support@getrelatio.com`. There is no human phone or live chat support mentioned (only the in-app *AI* chat for guidance).
* **SLAs & Escalations:** The site promises to get back "ASAP," but does not explicitly guarantee a specific hour-based SLA.
* **Self-Service Options:** An FAQ section, automated in-app cancellation, and an "AI chat support" feature available 24/7 to answer basic guidance questions.
* **Human vs. Automated:**
* *Automated:* In-app subscription cancellations, daily task curation, and AI chat support for basic relationship/app usage questions.
* *Human Required:* Billing disputes, technical bug escalations, and sensitive data deletion requests.



## 4. Edge Cases

Here are 10 realistic edge cases a support agent for Relatio might encounter:

1. **Domain: Technical Errors / Product Expectations**
* *Scenario:* A user asks the in-app "AI chat support" for a diagnosis regarding severe Erectile Dysfunction, and the AI refuses to answer or gives a generic disclaimer. The user emails support complaining the AI is "broken."
* *Why it's difficult:* Relatio explicitly states it is *not* a substitute for medical care or therapy. The agent must defend the AI's refusal to provide medical advice while keeping the user engaged with the natural exercises.
* *Customer Emotion:* Frustrated and embarrassed.


2. **Domain: Account Access / Privacy**
* *Scenario:* A user demands that Relatio support send an anonymous "readiness check" text to their ex, claiming the app promised to help them reconnect.
* *Why it's difficult:* The FAQ explicitly states: "We never contact anyone on your behalf." The agent must clarify that the app provides *scripts* and *guidance*, not a proxy messaging service.
* *Customer Emotion:* Annoyed and entitled.


3. **Domain: Payment Issues**
* *Scenario:* A user uninstalls the app out of grief after failing to win their ex back, assuming this cancels the subscription. They are billed 3 months later and demand a retroactive refund.
* *Why it's difficult:* App deletion does not cancel server-side or App Store subscriptions. The agent must explain the correct `Profile → Settings → Cancel` flow post-billing.
* *Customer Emotion:* Highly distressed and angry.


4. **Domain: Refunds**
* *Scenario:* A user demands a refund because the "Performance" module (kegels/breathwork) did not cure their premature ejaculation after 3 days.
* *Why it's difficult:* The FAQ notes "results vary" and it is an educational tool, not a medical cure. Handling a refund request tied to highly personal, failed sexual performance is delicate.
* *Customer Emotion:* Vulnerable, disappointed, and defensive.


5. **Domain: Account Access**
* *Scenario:* A couple is using the app together, but they have a massive real-life fight. One partner emails support demanding the other partner's account be locked or their shared data be erased immediately.
* *Why it's difficult:* Support cannot arbitrarily lock a second user's account without navigating strict privacy and account ownership policies, putting the agent in the middle of a domestic dispute.
* *Customer Emotion:* Hostile and urgent.


6. **Domain: Payment Issues**
* *Scenario:* A user cannot find the in-app cancel button because they subscribed via the Apple App Store, meaning Apple hides the billing management deep in the iOS settings.
* *Why it's difficult:* The agent must instruct the user to leave the Relatio app and navigate Apple's UI, which users frequently interpret as the company being scammy.
* *Customer Emotion:* Suspicious and impatient.


7. **Domain: Technical Errors**
* *Scenario:* A user is doing a 10-minute audio guided meditation for breakup healing, and the app crashes right at the emotional climax of the audio.
* *Why it's difficult:* The technical bug directly disrupted a highly vulnerable emotional moment. A standard "please clear your cache" response will feel incredibly tone-deaf.
* *Customer Emotion:* Upset, emotionally drained, and angry.


8. **Domain: Tariff & Plan Questions**
* *Scenario:* A user took the quiz for "Breakup Healing," purchased a 6-month plan, but halfway through gets back with their ex. They want to switch their curriculum to "Improve intimacy" but can't figure out how to reset the algorithm without paying again.
* *Why it's difficult:* The agent needs to help the user transition their account state/journey without requiring a new subscription, which may require backend intervention.
* *Customer Emotion:* Confused but optimistic.


9. **Domain: Refunds**
* *Scenario:* A user claims they accidentally subscribed to the annual plan instead of the monthly plan because the pricing screen was "deceptive."
* *Why it's difficult:* The agent must investigate if the purchase was made via web or App Store. If App Store, the agent has zero power to issue the refund and must send the user to Apple support.
* *Customer Emotion:* Panicked about a large unexpected charge.



## 5. Agent Pitfall Areas

1. **Crossing the Line into Medical/Therapeutic Advice**
* *The Pitfall:* A user emails saying they are deeply depressed about their breakup and feeling hopeless. The agent tries to be overly empathetic and offers psychological advice or counseling phrasing.
* *Why it fails:* Relatio is strictly *not* a substitute for professional therapy or medical care. Agents must use firm disclaimers and recommend licensed clinicians for mental-health or safety concerns to avoid massive liability.


2. **Mishandling Sensitive Reconnection Scenarios**
* *The Pitfall:* A user complains that their ex blocked them after they used a Relatio "consent-first message script," and the agent apologizes by saying, "I'm sorry our script didn't work, maybe try texting them this instead..."
* *Why it fails:* The agent is taking responsibility for the ex's reaction and encouraging further unwanted contact. Agents must reinforce that reconnection is only for when it is "healthy and welcome" and pivot to the "healing and moving forward" modules.


3. **Treating Intimacy/ED Complaints as Standard Tech Bugs**
* *The Pitfall:* A user writes in frustrated that the pelvic floor exercises aren't clearly explained in the video, and the agent responds with a cheery, generic macro: "Oh no! Have you tried restarting your device?"
* *Why it fails:* The user is discussing a highly sensitive, vulnerable topic (ED/sexual performance). A bubbly, generic tech-support tone will feel humiliating and dismissive.


4. **Failing to Distinguish App Store vs. Direct Billing**
* *The Pitfall:* A user emails asking to cancel. The agent manually cancels their web account but forgets to check if they purchased via Google Play.
* *Why it fails:* The user continues to be billed by Google, resulting in chargebacks, angry reviews, and a loss of trust. The agent must verify the billing origin before confirming cancellation.
